## :telescope: Spacetime Foam: Reviews, Models & Observational Tests

- Probing Spacetime Foam with Extragalactic Sources of High-Energy Photons (2022) — Ng & Perlman. Reviews proposals to constrain foam models via phase incoherence of wavefronts from quasars and GRBs, including image blurring and fringe visibility. This is a direct precursor from the paper's Ref. [4] authors and is not cited.

- Spacetime foam: a review (2022) — S. Carlip. Comprehensive review of Wheeler's foam program, theoretical implementations, and observational tests. This is the paper's Ref. [3].

- Holographic Quantum Foam: Theoretical Underpinnings and Observational Evidence (2026) — Steinbring & Ng. Argues GRB221009A observations are consistent with holographic foam blurring of the point-spread function. A very recent, closely related paper not yet cited.

- Astronomical Image Blurring from Transversely Correlated Quantum Gravity Fluctuations (2024) — Lee, Zurek & Chen. Derives transverse correlation functions for a specific foam model (the "pixellon") and shows that long-range transverse correlations strongly suppress image blurring. Directly relevant to the paper's treatment of the transverse coherence function Φ(b/r⊥).

- Quantum Area Fluctuations from Gravitational Phase Space (2025) — Ciambelli, He & Zurek. Derives area variance ∝ area for causal diamonds from quantum gravity phase space — a theoretical underpinning related to the Ng–Perlman scaling.

## :ocean: GW Phase Diffusion Through Stochastic Spacetime

- Universality and Falsifiability of Quantum Spacetime Decoherence: A Gauge-Invariant Framework for Gravitational-Wave Phase Diffusion (2025) — Cang & Wang. Derives a universality theorem for phase diffusion of GWs through stochastic quantum spacetime, analogous to the paper's optical coherence framework. Discusses achromatic phase variance accumulation and Bayesian tests with LIGO/LISA. Directly relevant to §V F.

## :zap: LIV & Time-of-Flight Tests (the paper's "second channel")

These papers collectively update or extend the Fermi/H.E.S.S. constraints the paper cites as Refs. [11, 12]:

- Stringent Tests of Lorentz Invariance Violation from LHAASO Observations of GRB 221009A (2024) — LHAASO Collaboration. Sets leading limits on linear and quadratic LIV using the brightest-ever GRB at TeV energies. Citation count: 28.

- Lorentz Invariance Violation from Gamma-Ray Bursts (2025) — Song & Ma. Updates LIV energy scale from multi-event Fermi/MAGIC/LHAASO data.

- Constraints on quantum gravity and the photon mass from gamma ray bursts (2021) — Bartlett et al. Derives constraints using BATSE spectral lag data with forward-modeled time delays. 20 citations.

- Exploring Lorentz Invariance Violation from Ultra-high-energy Gamma Rays Observed by LHAASO (2021) — LHAASO Collaboration. First PeV-photon LIV limits, improving previous results by an order of magnitude.

## :star2: Stellar Intensity Interferometry (HBT experiments cited as Refs. [15, 16])

These experimental papers on the revival of optical intensity interferometry are directly relevant to the observational proposals in §VI:

- An Angular Diameter Measurement of β UMa via Stellar Intensity Interferometry with the VERITAS Observatory (2024) — Acharyya et al. (VERITAS). The most recent published VERITAS-SII science result; extends the program of Ref. [16].

- Performance and first measurements of the MAGIC Stellar Intensity Interferometer (2024) — MAGIC Collaboration. Reports 22 stellar diameter measurements with MAGIC-SII. 17 citations.

- Simultaneous Two Colour Intensity Interferometry with H.E.S.S. (2024) — Vogel et al. First dual-waveband simultaneous HBT measurements on stars — the multi-wavelength dimension is directly relevant to the paper's achromaticity discriminant.

- Stellar intensity interferometry in the photon-counting regime (2025) — Guerin et al. State-of-the-art review of SII signal-to-noise theory in the photon-counting regime.

- Increasing the sensitivity of stellar intensity interferometry with optical telescopes: First laboratory test of spectral multiplexing (2024) — Tolila et al. Demonstrates spectral multiplexing to improve SII SNR — relevant to the broadband HBT proposal in §VII.

- Probing Stellar Kinematics with the Time-Asymmetric Hanbury Brown and Twiss Effect (2025) — Stanic et al. Shows that temporal HBT correlation asymmetry probes stellar kinematics — a new use of the same g(2)(τ) observable the paper develops.

- Stellar intensity interferometry of Vega in photon counting mode (2021) — Zampieri et al. First photon-counting g(2) measurement of a star at km baseline. 16 citations.

- Validating the Angular Sizes of Red Clump Stars with Intensity Interferometry (2026) — Kim & Kaiser. Co-authored by the paper's first author; directly relevant companion paper on HBT with CTA/CTAO.
